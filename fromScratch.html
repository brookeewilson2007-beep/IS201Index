What is a bassoon?

The bassoon is a woodwind instrument that plays in the tenor and bass ranges. It is often considered the cello of band. It originates from two styles: French and German. It is a double reed instrument, like the oboe. There are also other versions of the bassoon, the most prominent variation being the contrabassoon, which is bigger and pitched an octave lower than the standard bassoon.

Why the bassoon?

My story with the bassoon begins in 5th grade. I went to the band hall to try out which instrument I would be playing in band for the next school year. My mom played flute, so for years, I thought I would too. That was not the case. I tried a bassoon reed, and it felt right. I came in again a couple days later to try the actual instrument, and it was like Harry Potter trying out his wand - the bassoon chose me.

Parts of a Bassoon:

  1. The Reed
    • Made of two pieces of cane stuck together with wire and string
    • Can be played on its own, but it's very buzzy
  2. The Bocal
    • The curved, metal piece that connects the reed to the rest of the instrument
  3. The Body
    • Wing Joint: Connects the bocal to the boot
    • Boot: The bottom of the bassoon where the seat strap connects
    • Long Joint: Connects the boot to the bell
    • Bell: The top of the instrument that projects sound
